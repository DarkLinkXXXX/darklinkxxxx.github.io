An Introduction

Last September or so, I applied at the local community college, and I was so excited. I had been working outside on my traffic control job for at least over a year at this point, and I wasn’t at all looking forward to working in the winter.

And yet, when the quarter started and the going started getting tough… I folded. I stopped turning in homework, and I dropped my math and english class, leaving only my college success class. What a waste of money…

This isn’t the first time something like this has happened with me. When I was in Sophmore year of highschool, I got just good enough grades to get into a program where I could go to community college and have the college credits count as high school credits, and it would be funded by the state. I was at least passing in some classes, but I failed out of college after my second quarter, and it was back to high school with me. How demoralizing that was…

I’m not exactly sure what’s wrong with me, other than my bad habits. I’m not great at committing myself to a project, or a class, or an assignment for any long period of time, and I’m trying to change that about myeslf. I’m gonna go back to working and make up for the college money I lost, and my car is on its last legs so I also have that to look forward to…

I’m gonna try to establish good study habits between now and next fall by studying a subject on my own for an hour or so after work. It’s not gonna be easy, but I’m gonna try forcing myself to write about my progress and/or what I have learned here every week in order to motivate myself to have something to show. Wish me luck!

— Nonya Bizness
